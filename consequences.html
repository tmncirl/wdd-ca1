Consequences

Climate change will eventually impact all aspects of our daily lives. Although we may think that the consequences are far in the future, we are already beginning to see the effects today.

We are seeing an increase in severe weather events around the globe such as droughts and floods. These will only become more frequent and severe as the planet warms up. Climate change will impact our food, health, water, enviornment, infrastructure and more.

The most well known effect of climate change will be the sea level rising, which will flood many coastal areas around the world.

Heat, droughts and extreme weather will make farming much harder in most of the world. Crops that are suitable for growing in certain areas will have to move to stay in the same climates. Some of the worst impacts can be mitigated by using greenhouses and adjacent technologies but food production will still be decreased massively. A warmer climate will also mean more water is needed to grow crops which will put a strain on the decreasing supply of water worldwide.





Climate change will lead to increased droughts but also increased flooding. Weather events are getting more frequent and severe as the planet warms up. These factors will put strain on our already struggling water resouces. This could be disastrous as it will be much hotter, meaning people will need more water than is currently available.
The ocean also absorbs carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, which is making it more acidic over time. This could kill off or disrupt a lot of marine life.





The environment will be affected heavily by climate change. As the world continues to warm, many species will be forced to migrate to keep in a climate that suits them. This could lead to the extinction of many species.
Many habitats would also not survive heavy climate change such as coral reefs and some animals or plants would not have the time they need to adapt, and would simply die off.





Weather events are getting increasingly frequent and severe, which will lead to death during and in the aftermath of these events. Hurricanes and wildfires have already become much deadlier in the US. Flooding can spread waterborne diseases, and disease carrying pests will move into new habitats, further spreading diseases to locations they have not been before.





The warming of the planet is affecting the seasons and weather patterns that have been stable for a long time. Certain plants are unable to grow as they normally have, or in the case of food are lower quality. This will lead to the loss of bio diversity.
Warming will also lead to an increase of desertifcation. This will cause further warming as nature will not be able to regulate conditions and participate in cycles that cool the planet.
